Artist Statement

Art is like a Rubik’s Cube: a colorful, deceptively simple puzzle with multi-layered components. As a chronic overthinker, I enjoy the strange sort of peace I feel as I focus on one of my paintings. Art is one of the few things I do wholly for myself. It’s not for school, or for my parents, or for the dreams I feel like I need to have. No, it’s a time to live in the moment: an effort to understand the world around me.

While I sketch and paint people, animals, and objects, what I’m really interested in is everything not immediately obvious in my work -- ideas, systems, and emotions. With the advent of photography, realism has almost become redundant. Why show the same thing a camera captures, when the paper offers so much freedom? As I’ve honed my technical skills in multiple mediums, my style has grown increasingly experimental. I love playing with contrasting colors and textures; my fascination with tension creates dynamic, often vivid pieces that seek to capture the small nuances of the ordinary.

My many thoughts contribute to my style, which is an impatient mix of cross-hatching and lines on paper or quick brushstrokes on canvas. I’m drawn to big cats and birds: cats because of their lithe strength, and birds because of their sleek feathers. I love the large brushstrokes of the Impressionist movement, but I’m often contained to small strokes because of my subject matter; creating the texture of feathers or fur requires a careful hand and a keen eye. Realism teaches me patience, while experimentation is an outlet for my creativity. I’m constantly trying to maintain that balance of substance and personality.
